Remembrance Day sometimes known informally as Poppy Day owing to the tradition of the remembrance poppy is a memorial day observed in Commonwealth member states since the end of the First World War to remember the members of their armed forces who have died in the line of duty. Following a tradition inaugurated by King George the day is also marked by war remembrances in many non-Commonwealth countries. Remembrance Day is observed in zero. November in most countries to recall the end of hostilities of First World War on that date.
